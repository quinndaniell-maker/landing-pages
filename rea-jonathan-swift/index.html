Where this started

“Could Airtable complement Jira — a more flexible operational layer for how product actually runs, day to day?”

When we spoke last year, that was the thread you kept pulling on: not replacing anything, but whether Airtable could support how REA's product organisation actually operates — especially around visibility across teams, coordination as work crosses boundaries, and processes that never stop evolving.

It stuck with me. This page is my attempt at a specific answer — grounded in how REA already works, not a generic pitch.

What's already true at REA

You've already done the hard part.

From the outside, a few things are clear. REA runs something like 70 cross-functional teams in tribes across five or six business units, with a genuine “you build it, you run it” culture. And you've made a deliberate bet: Jira and Jira Product Discovery as the single, trusted, real-time source of truth for delivery — the kind of deck-free visibility most orgs only talk about.

So this isn't an argument to change any of that. At your scale, the interesting gap usually isn't tracking delivery — you've solved that. It's the operational connective tissue around delivery: the coordination, portfolio, intake and stakeholder work that today probably lives in spreadsheets, Confluence and side channels, because it was never shaped like a ticket.

In your words

Three places Airtable earns its place.

The same three you named — visibility, coordination, evolving processes. Here's what each looks like in practice.

01

Visibility beyond the backlog

Jira shows delivery. But leadership questions rarely stop there: how does this roadmap ladder to our bets, what is a launch actually waiting on, where are we against the OKRs? Airtable blends live Jira delivery data with everything that isn't a ticket — research, customer signals, GTM readiness, finance — into role-specific Interfaces. Your leaders get a live view built for them, without a Jira licence or a slide.

02

Coordination across the seams

The hardest work at your scale happens between teams and business units — launches with a dozen dependencies, roadmaps that span multiple tribes. Ticket-shaped tools handle that awkwardly. Airtable is a natural coordination surface: linked records for dependencies, shared launch calendars, one intake that routes each request to the right tribe's Jira project. Coordination lives in one place; execution stays in Jira.

03

Processes that evolve in days

Your operating model keeps moving — new prioritisation frameworks, portfolio reviews, experiment tracking, intake flows. Modelling each new process into Jira's schema is slow and permanent. In Airtable, product ops reshapes a workflow in an afternoon — new fields, views, automations, AI steps — with no engineering ticket. The flexibility is the point: it bends as your process does.

How it fits

Additive, not parallel.

Each tool stays authoritative for its audience. Jira and Jira Product Discovery own engineering delivery and discovery. Airtable owns the operational layer above them. They stay in sync automatically — nobody changes how they work.

Why Airtable, specifically

Not a spreadsheet workaround.

Airtable ProductCentral is purpose-built for product operations — designed to align strategy and customer insight to execution — with native, enterprise-grade Jira Cloud sync. It's flexible enough to match how your teams already work, and structured enough to be a real system of record.

It's also the pattern a lot of digital-native product orgs are converging on: keep engineering in Jira, run the operations in Airtable. Given where REA already is, that convergence feels less like a change of direction and more like the natural next layer.
